# The Archetype Wheel (12-Phase Cognitive Field Model)

The Archetype Wheel describes twelve distinct cognitive‚Äìbehavioural configurations generated by the interactions of the Four Axes of cognition.  
Each archetype represents a **stable phase** within the larger dynamic field of human cognition.

The Archetype Wheel is not a personality typing system.  
Instead, it models **how cognition organizes itself in patterned configurations** depending on context, development, and internal state.  
Individuals move fluidly between archetypes across time ‚Äî a process formalized in the Personality Wave Model.

---

# üß≠ Overview

Each archetype arises from the interaction of **two poles from two different axes**.  
Because the axes form a 4-dimensional system, there are exactly **12 unique pairings**, producing 12 archetypal modes.

These modes are:

1. Seer  
2. Mentor  
3. Healer  
4. Nurturer  
5. Advocate  
6. Rebel  
7. Creator  
8. Driver  
9. Builder  
10. Architect  
11. Analyst  
12. Sage  

Each one reflects a coherent cognitive pattern with predictable strengths, stress responses, and developmental tendencies.

# üß¨ The 12 Archetypes (Brief Definitions)

Below are concise, neutral descriptions suitable for public reference.

### **1. Seer (Vision + Logic)**
Pattern recognition, abstraction, conceptual insight, intuitive analysis.

### **2. Mentor (Vision + Value)**
Guidance, inspiration, articulating meaning, helping others see potential.

### **3. Healer (Value + Reality)**
Understanding emotional context, grounding meaning in lived experience.

### **4. Nurturer (Value + Care)**
Supportive stability, tending to needs, facilitating harmony.

### **5. Advocate (Care + Value)**
Championing individuals or causes, ensuring fairness and protection.

### **6. Rebel (Action + Vision)**
Disruption, challenging assumptions, pushing boundaries, initiating change.

### **7. Creator (Innovation + Vision)**
Creative ideation, synthesizing possibilities, generating novel approaches.

### **8. Driver (Action + Logic)**
Efficiency, execution, decisive problem-solving, operational clarity.

### **9. Builder (Action + Structure)**
Constructive implementation, organizing resources, achieving tangible results.

### **10. Architect (Structure + Innovation)**
Design thinking, systems creation, structural creativity, strategic planning.

### **11. Analyst (Structure + Logic)**
Precision, evaluation, categorization, rigorous reasoning.

### **12. Sage (Logic + Structure)**
Synthesis of knowledge, principle-driven clarity, long-view understanding.

# üìê Why 12 Archetypes?

Because:

- there are **4 cognitive axes**,  
- each with **2 poles**,  
- and archetypes emerge from the **pairing of two poles from separate axes**,  
- the system necessarily produces **12 unique combinations**.

This 12-phase model captures:

- diversity of cognitive patterns  
- dynamic movement between configurations  
- predictable developmental sequences  
- stable and unstable configurations under stress  

The system is **structural**, not cultural.  
It does not depend on typology traditions, personality categories, or value systems.

# üîÑ Relationship to the Personality Wave Model

The Archetype Wheel is **static** ‚Äî it shows the positions.

The Personality Wave Model is **dynamic** ‚Äî it shows how individuals:

- shift between archetypes  
- expand and contract in cognitive flexibility  
- navigate stress vs coherence states  
- follow predictable paths of development  

Together, they form the core of the Dynamic Field Model.
