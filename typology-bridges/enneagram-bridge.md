# Enneagram Bridge  
The Enneagram Bridge explains how patterns found in the Enneagram system can be
loosely related to the Dynamic Field Model (DFM).  
This is not a typing tool, diagnostic mechanism, or personality classification
system.  
Instead, it highlights conceptual overlaps and translation points between:

- Enneagram motivational patterns, and  
- DFM‚Äôs axis dynamics, wave patterns, and archetypal modes.

DFM and the Enneagram serve different purposes, but understanding how they
intersect can help users familiar with the Enneagram orient themselves within
DFM‚Äôs cognitive structure.

# üß≠ How the Enneagram and DFM Differ

### **1. Enneagram: motivational structure**
The Enneagram describes:
- core motivations  
- emotional strategies  
- defensive patterns  
- growth and stress directions  

### **2. DFM: cognitive-field structure**
DFM models:
- cognitive tensions (the axes)  
- temporary modes (archetypes)  
- movement (waves)  
- intelligence integration  

### **3. Enneagram is identity-oriented; DFM is state-oriented**
Enneagram types describe psychological patterns of self-structure.  
DFM avoids identity and describes **real-time cognitive dynamics**.

### **4. DFM does not map one-to-one to Enneagram types**
The mapping is relational, not categorical.
