The MBTI Bridge explains how patterns described in the Myers‚ÄìBriggs Type
Indicator (MBTI) can be meaningfully translated into the structural framework

This document does *not* evaluate, endorse, type, or diagnose any individuals.

Instead, it shows where MBTI concepts **overlap**, **diverge**, and **map

# üß≠ How DFM and MBTI Differ

### **1. DFM is dynamic; MBTI is categorical**
- MBTI places individuals into one of 16 types.  
- DFM describes **movement** within a cognitive field, not fixed categories.

### **2. DFM models tensions; MBTI models preferences**
- MBTI highlights preferred modes of perception and judgment.  
- DFM maps cognitive polarity axes and wave-like transitions.

### **3. DFM avoids personality typing**
Unlike MBTI, the Dynamic Field Model:
- does not classify  
- does not assign a type  
- does not infer stable traits  
- does not offer personality predictions  

DFM instead provides a *topology* ‚Äî a cognitive map.

## **1. The Four Axes and MBTI Dichotomies**

### **Logic ‚Üî Value**
Relates loosely to:
- **Thinking (T)** ‚Äî logic, structure  
- **Feeling (F)** ‚Äî values, relational meaning  

### **Vision ‚Üî Reality**
Relates loosely to:
- **Intuition (N)** ‚Äî patterns, possibilities  
- **Sensing (S)** ‚Äî concreteness, detail  

### **Innovation ‚Üî Action**
Shares features with:
- **Perceiving (P)** ‚Äî openness, exploration  
- **Judging (J)** ‚Äî decisiveness, structure  

### **Structure ‚Üî Care**
Has conceptual overlaps with:
- **Judging (J)** again (structuring tendencies)  
- **Feeling (F)** again (relational care)  

*Note:* MBTI dichotomies are preference indicators, not cognitive tensions, so the mapping is approximate.

## **2. MBTI Cognitive Functions and DFM Archetypes**

DFM archetypes describe **temporary modes**, not trait functions.  
However, some parallels arise:

- **Seer** resembles intuitive‚Äìanalytical abstraction (often linked with *Ni/Ne + Ti*).  
- **Analyst** resembles rigorous evaluation (similar to *Ti/Te* modes).  
- **Nurturer** resembles relational stabilization (parallels with *Si + Fe*).  
- **Creator** resembles ideational expansion (*Ne* characteristics).  
- **Driver** resembles decisive execution (*Te*-like).  
- **Healer** resembles emotional grounding (*Fi* patterns).  
- **Architect** resembles structured design (*Ni + Te* synergy).  

These parallels are observational ‚Äî not definitional.

---

## **3. Wave Movement vs. Function Stacks**

MBTI proposes a hierarchical stack of functions.  
DFM proposes **wave dynamics** instead:

- Under stress, people may move toward familiar cognitive modes.  
- Under coherence, they may expand into less familiar ones.  
- Movement is shaped by axis tensions, not function order.  

In this sense, DFM explains *why* certain functions feel easier or harder, without framing them as inherent traits.
