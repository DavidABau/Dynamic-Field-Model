# DFM Interface  
## A Human Guide to Realignment Under Pressure

This guide is not about winning arguments, expressing feelings, or solving problems.

It is about **restoring alignment first**.

Nothing meaningful can happen while alignment is lost.

---

## The Core Principle

When people become misaligned, **communication stops working**.

Logic fails.  
Empathy fails.  
Good intentions fail.

The only task that matters is **realignment**.

At least one person must stop trying to communicate *content* and instead stabilise the **field between them**.

---

## The Four-Step Realignment Protocol

This is a **minimum viable sequence** for restoring alignment under pressure.

### 1. Recognition — *Notice the Drift*

Misalignment shows up before words do.

Common signs:
- conversations looping or escalating
- feeling unheard, rushed, or defensive
- one person pushing while the other withdraws
- “We’re talking, but nothing is landing”

**Recognition does not assign blame.**  
It simply names reality:

> *“We are no longer aligned.”*

That recognition alone is a regulating act.

---

### 2. Pause — *Stop Adding Signal*

Once drift is recognised, **stop transmitting content**.

Do not:
- explain
- correct
- justify
- clarify
- or escalate emotional detail

Pause is not withdrawal.
Pause is **containment**.

This can be explicit:
> “Let’s pause for a moment.”

Or implicit:
- slowing speech
- fewer words
- softer tone
- stillness

The goal is to stop adding noise.

---

### 3. Regulation — *Stabilise Yourself First*

Realignment always starts **internally**.

You cannot regulate the relationship until you regulate yourself.

Effective regulation under pressure is simple and physical:
- slow your breathing
- relax shoulders and jaw
- lower vocal intensity
- ground attention in the body

You are not trying to feel calm.
You are trying to feel **present and stable**.

Even partial regulation changes the field.

---

### 4. Contact — *Restore Human Connection*

Only after regulation comes **contact**.

Contact is not problem-solving.
It is not agreement.
It is not explanation.

Contact is **re-establishing safety and recognition**.

Examples:
- “I’m here.”
- “I’m not trying to fight you.”
- “We’re on the same side.”
- “I care about this.”

Tone matters more than words.

Contact tells the other nervous system:
> *“You are not alone in this moment.”*

This is the turning point.

---

## Why This Works

Misalignment is not primarily cognitive.  
It is **state-based**.

When one person:
- recognises drift
- pauses escalation
- regulates themselves
- and offers contact

…the other person’s system almost always softens.

Alignment spreads **asymmetrically**.
One regulated person can stabilise two.

---

## What Comes *After* Realignment

Only after alignment is restored can you safely move into:
- sharing feelings
- explaining perspectives
- solving problems
- making decisions

If alignment drops again, **return to the protocol**.

There is no penalty for repeating steps.

---

## Common Mistakes

- Trying to explain *why* the other person is upset  
- Pushing for resolution while regulation is absent  
- Treating pause as avoidance  
- Waiting for the other person to go first  

Realignment is an **act of leadership**, not concession.

---

## A Simple Rule to Remember

> **No alignment → no conversation.**

Everything else can wait.

---

## Final Orientation

This guide is not about being right.
It is about preserving connection under pressure.

If you can recognise drift and realign even once,
you have already changed the trajectory of the interaction.

That is enough to begin.
