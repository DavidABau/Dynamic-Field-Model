# Decision-Making in the Dynamic Field Model

Decision-making is one of the clearest ways the cognitive field expresses
itself.  
The Dynamic Field Model (DFM) provides a structured way to understand how
decisions form, how they become distorted, and how they can be improved through
balanced axis engagement and integrative intelligence.

This model does not prescribe what decisions individuals *should* make.  
Instead, it offers a framework for understanding **how** decisions are formed
and how to support clarity, coherence, and healthy choice-making.

---

# üß≠ Core Principles of Decision-Making in DFM

### **1. Decisions emerge from axis balance**
Every decision is shaped by the Four Axes:

- **Logic ‚Üî Value**  
  (Is the decision true? Is it meaningful?)

- **Vision ‚Üî Reality**  
  (What is possible? What is actually happening?)

- **Innovation ‚Üî Action**  
  (What could be done? What can be implemented now?)

- **Structure ‚Üî Care**  
  (What stabilizes the system? What supports people?)

Balanced decisions arise when multiple poles contribute rather than one pole
dominating.

---

### **2. Decisions reflect current archetypal mode**
Each archetype has characteristic decision-making tendencies.

Examples:
- **Seer:** pattern-based insight  
- **Builder:** practical implementation  
- **Advocate:** fairness and protection  
- **Analyst:** rigorous evaluation  
- **Rebel:** disruption or change initiation  

These modes are temporary ‚Äî not fixed traits ‚Äî and influence how decisions are
framed.

---

### **3. Decisions shift according to wave patterns**
Stress or coherence states affect decision-making:

- **Coherence waves:** broader perspective, clearer reasoning, balanced input  
- **Contraction waves:** narrowed thinking, rigidity, emotional reactivity  
- **Oscillation waves:** indecision, overcorrection, inconsistent choices  

Recognizing wave patterns helps individuals understand *why* a decision feels
difficult or pressured.

- **Value-dominant:** emotionally-charged decisions with unclear rationale  

---

**Vision contributes:**
- imagination

- conceptual framing  

**Reality contributes:**

- ‚ÄúWhat could this become?‚Äù

# üîÑ Decision-Making Dynamics Across the Archetype Wheel

Because each archetype emerges from a unique pairing of axes, each brings
strengths and blind spots:

Examples:

- **Creator:** strong at generating options, weaker at follow-through  
- **Driver:** decisive and efficient, may overlook nuance  
- **Healer:** emotionally attuned, may underweight objective data  
- **Architect:** strong systems thinking, may miss relational cues  
- **Sage:** high-level conceptual clarity, risk of overabstraction  

The goal is **integration**, not preference for one archetypal approach.

---

# üß¨ The Role of Integrative Intelligence

Integrative Intelligence (the 7th dimension) is the key to high-quality
decision-making.

It allows individuals to:
- coordinate multiple axes  
- hold conflicting information simultaneously  
- integrate emotional and logical signals  
- track short-term and long-term consequences  
- maintain coherence under pressure  

In mature decision-making, no single axis dominates the field.

# üîß Practical Decision-Making Framework (DFM-Based)

A simple, universal process:

### **1. Clarify the Situation**
Identify which axes are currently activated.

### **2. Gather Inputs from Each Axis**
Ask:
- What is true? (Logic)  
- What matters? (Value)  
- What is possible? (Vision)  
- What is real now? (Reality)  
- What are the options? (Innovation)  
- What is doable? (Action)  
- What stabilizes the system? (Structure)  
- Who is affected and how? (Care)  

### **3. Identify Current Wave State**
Is it contraction, expansion, or oscillation?

### **4. Adjust Pace Accordingly**
- Slow down under contraction  
- Expand context in coherence  
- Narrow focus under analysis overload  

### **5. Integrate, Then Decide**
Bring all axes together into a coherent synthesis rather than allowing one pole
to dominate.

It is a **framework for clarity**, not a prescription for choices.
